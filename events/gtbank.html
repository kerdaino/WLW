GTCO Fashion Weekend 2025: Lagos Gets Ready for Style

Written by: Maryam Opoola

Lagos is already buzzing again. You can almost feel it in the air, fabrics being cut, designs coming to life, and fashion lovers getting ready for one of the city’s biggest weekends. Yes, GTCO Fashion Weekend 2025 is around the corner, and this year, it promises to be even bigger. The event will take place on November 8th and 9th at the GT Centre in Victoria Island, Lagos. For two full days, the space will transform into a fashion village filled with runway shows, masterclasses, exhibitions, and a lively marketplace where creativity meets commerce.

GTCO Fashion Weekend isn’t just another fashion event. It’s a celebration of African style, talent, and enterprise. Since it started in 2016, it has become a stage where designers, fashion entrepreneurs, stylists, and lovers of good design come together to share ideas and explore what’s next for African fashion. This year’s focus is on innovation, sustainability, and inclusion themes that have continued to shape the global fashion conversation. Expect to see a mix of bold runway looks, handmade pieces, and contemporary designs that show how African fashion keeps evolving while staying true to its roots.

One of the highlights every year is the GTCO Marketplace. Here, upcoming designers and small fashion brands get to showcase and sell their collections directly to visitors. It’s the kind of space where many new brands get discovered. From ready-to-wear to handmade accessories, the marketplace captures the real heartbeat of Lagos fashion: raw, vibrant, and full of stories.

The masterclasses are another major part of the weekend. Some of the most respected names in the fashion and creative industries will be there to share insights on brand growth, digital storytelling, sustainability, and the business of fashion. Whether you’re a designer, stylist, or just starting out, there’s something to learn.

As for the runway shows, they’re always the highlight. You’ll see collections that bring together tradition and modernity, from reimagined indigenous fabrics to sleek, contemporary silhouettes. It’s fashion that feels global but remains proudly African.

If you’re planning to attend, come early, wear your best street style, and keep your camera ready because GTCO Fashion Weekend is as much about the people watching as it is about the clothes. And the best part? Admission to the event is free; you only need to register ahead on the GTCO Fashion Weekend website. At its core, this event is a reminder of what makes Lagos fashion special, its energy, creativity, and unstoppable drive. Year after year, GTCO Fashion Weekend continues to prove that Africa’s fashion scene isn’t waiting for validation; it’s already leading the way.
